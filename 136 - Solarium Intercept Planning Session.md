# 136 - Solarium Intercept Planning Session
On Thursday, June 22, 1843, at approximately 7:41 AM in the Solarium of the Palace of Loth Logan, Ryan Adame proposed killing Thomas Greaves as a strategic option, shifting the group's stance from defensive to offensive. The room—containing Charlotte, Florence, Estra, Cora, Claire, and Althena—fell into a weighted silence. Estra evaluated the proposal on tactical merit, noting it would force Loren to recalibrate every assumption about their willingness to operate inside the rules. Charlotte stress-tested the logic, arguing a dead agent yields no intelligence while a captured one could expose Loren's palace network, but acknowledged through the bond that she recognized Ryan's cold resolve. Florence expressed quiet concern about the moral weight of execution outside combat. Cora warned that Loren escalates when challenged. Claire, gripping the teapot, noted that whoever kills must live with it afterward. Althena reframed the question, stating she is not a goddess of war but of family, and the issue is not whether killing is a sin but what the killing serves to protect.

At 7:42 AM, Mina returned with Crown Princess Cornelia vi Relani, who immediately sensed the room's tension. Ryan introduced Cornelia to Althena with characteristic irreverence, calling her 'God with divine tits.' Cornelia's composure cracked for three seconds before she laughed in startled disbelief. She then revealed critical intelligence: Thomas Greaves has been watching Queen Maya since April 1843, not Prince Charlie, with twice-weekly dead drops. This indicated target assessment rather than mere reconnaissance. Cornelia confirmed Greaves works the east wing from noon to midnight in a steward's uniform with a forgettable face. Charlotte insisted any killing must be framed as defense of the crown to maintain legitimacy, not merely sending a message. Estra established clear thresholds: kill only if Greaves resists, tries to run, or has already passed critical intelligence. Claire asked what happens if he cooperates; Estra confirmed they would not kill a cooperative prisoner. Althena observed they are building a cage of rules around an act that resists rules—harder than killing and more important.

At 7:43 AM, Ryan identified a recurring pattern of turning Loren's agents for minimal intelligence that only helps for twelve to twenty-four hours before finding another mole. He argued Greaves has been following them since Day 1 and watching Maya even longer, making him ride-or-die Loren. Cornelia confirmed Greaves has been with Loren for twelve years with no family, no hostages, and no leverage—he is a true believer and disciple, not a coerced asset. Mina called him a disciple, not an asset. Florence concluded he cannot be turned. Cora noted the decision simplified: extract intelligence at the dead drop tonight, then decide whether he leaves the courtyard alive. Althena acknowledged Ryan is breaking the cycle, noting he now has power and choices, and is choosing to use them differently. Florence found humor in Althena accepting the 'divine tits' compliment. Cornelia muttered she needed more than tea.

At 7:45 AM, Cornelia sat and Ryan asked directly whether Loren had leverage over her as a child or whether she 'flipped him over for the love of the game.' Cornelia confessed both were true: her mother told her she was a placeholder princess, and Loren offered her relevance and significance at age ten. The price was pretending she did not know Ryan and forgetting him when told. She admitted she never forgot—remembering his toy soldier, his laugh, and that he was the first person who made her feel like she mattered. She traded that for Loren's approval anyway and only flipped when Ryan returned fifteen years later without recognizing her. Althena condemned Loren's pattern of treating children as tools, stating children are not assets or leverage. Claire returned with tea and brandy, offering both.

At 7:46 AM, Ryan absolved Cornelia entirely, reframing her as a manipulated child and comparing her to Priti. He declared they have more important issues than determining whether Cornelia is a Loren agent. Cornelia sat in stunned silence, then noted he is either the most forgiving or most pragmatic person she has met. Charlotte said he is both. Estra, Florence, and Mina acknowledged Ryan's pattern of collecting people Loren broke and putting them back together. Cornelia provided tactical intelligence: Greaves takes his meal break at exactly 8 PM every night, always in the kitchen courtyard, and the dead drop exchange happens during that window. He has never missed it in three months. Cora noted predictability is exploitable. Estra noted true believers are the hardest to turn but easiest to predict.

At 7:48 AM, Ryan proposed personally confronting Greaves at the dead drop. Cornelia called it either very brave or very stupid. Estra supported the plan, noting the lost prince personally removing a threat to the Queen becomes legitimacy and a story told for generations. Mina voiced concern about Greaves fighting back given his twelve years of experience. Charlotte assigned tactical roles: Estra covers the kitchen courtyard sightlines and escape routes before 8 PM, Mina guards the east corridor, Florence remains on standby as the fastest responder, and the solarium is prepared for interrogation. Cornelia confirmed Greaves will hesitate if confronted by Ryan alone, not perceiving him as an immediate threat. Cora noted the solarium's psychological advantages: soundproof, single entrance, no outside windows, disorienting for a bound prisoner. Claire asked what happens if Greaves will not talk; Estra answered that if he will not talk, he is not a prisoner but a threat, and threats get removed. Althena observed they are planning with every contingency, threshold, and justification—trying to be just in a situation that does not permit justice.
